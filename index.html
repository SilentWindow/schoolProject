Association for Computing Machinery (ACM)
Code of Conduct


ACM Code of Conduct dictates that we will be…

  • Honest and trustworthy.
  • Be fair and not discriminate.
  • Respect privacy.
  • Maintain high standards of professionalism.
  • Use care when modifying or retiring systems.
  • Treat code violations as unacceptable in the company culture.

Workplace Ethics


If you notice something suspicious, inform someone in your IT department

  • Always adhere to the Code of Conduct
  • Don't succumb to peer pressure, just because others are doing it doesnt make it right
  • If your supervisors brush off the issue, take your complaint to management.

Best Password Practices


The best practices for keeping your password safe are:

  • Never write down your password
  • Make sure your password is at least 10 characters
  • Use uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and special characters
  • Never tell anyone your password
  • Change your password periodically

Email Security


To avoid being the victim of a phishing scam always:

  • Check the sender of the email and determine if they’re from your company or not
  • Look for company norms such as signatures and contact info, usually at end of an email
  • Don’t click any links or open any attachments from unverified senders (anyone you know to be outside of company)
  • Never reply personal or company info to an unverified sender
  • Things to look for:
    • Emails insisting urgent action
    • Spelling mistakes
    • Unfamiliar greetings
    • All caps or sporadic capitalization

Network Security


To keep our networks safe:

  • Only use company wifi network or VPN
  • Do not allow unauthorized users on your device
  • Do not disperse company wifi passwords to non-employees
  • Never leave your device open and unattended

Data Security


Data Security Best Practices:

  • Don’t leave devices unattended
    • Turn off devices when not in use
    • Log out of accounts when you’re done using them
  • Only download authorized applications
    • Downloading unverified files or programs can cause security vulnerabilities
    • Approve downloads from non-company sources with management
  • Do not use company devices for personal uses
    • This includes personal email, social media, and shopping for example
    • Accessing inappropriate content on company computer OR company time is not tolerated
  • Ensure all software is up-to-date
    • Turn on automatic operating system updates
    • Security software updates prevent currently emerging malware from infecting your devices
